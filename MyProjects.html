My Dissertation from my final year of my BSc (Hons) Computer Science degree which I completed at Heriot Watt University.
Formula 1 is a data-driven sport, with large amounts of data being gathered and analysed to give competitors the edge over one another to achieve the goal of winning World Championships. This project will explore graph databases and visualisation techniques to model the dataset in Neo4j, and explore the visualisations provided to analyse the dataset and discuss the advantages and disadvantages of using graph databases for analysis of the dataset.



Edinburgh Airport Flight Information for August 2017
Using some raw data containing information about flights which arrived or departed from Edinburgh Airport, I created a report analysing this data to produce a monthly summary of passenger flight information illustrating trends on passenger distributions, aircraft delays, seat load factors, pier service levels, and passenger distributions across departure gates and baggage reclaim belts. This data analysis has been carried out using Microsoft Excel, with the report being produced with Microsoft Word. Furthermore, I have created a visualisation of this raw data in PowerBI to provide another method of representing this data.
